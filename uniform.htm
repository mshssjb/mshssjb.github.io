Dress– and Uniform Regulations

Detailed here is a guide on how to wear some parts of your uniform.

For a more comprehensive guide, see the dress manual below (this is the latest newest 2nd edition!!!):

Dress Regulations Manual

Basic Uniform

(No. 4 Uniform)

Parts

  • Beret
    • Beret pin / cap badge
  • Shirt
    • Shoulder Flashes
    • Rank Marks (on shoulder straps/epaulettes)
    • Brassard
    • CPBS badges (on brassard)
    • Other breastpocket badges
  • Belt
  • Pants
  • Boots

Appearance

  • Ironed, sleeves must have obvious ironing crease line
  • Boots toe-box polished
  • Star on shoulder-strap/epaulette pin faces outwards
  • Shoulder-strap pin covers over rank mark
  • Shirt tucked in
  • Beret stowed under right epaulette under cover

Shoulder Flashes

(Also known as 'shoulder patches' or 'unit patches')

This should be worn aligned to the ironing line on your uniform sleeve.
Ensure the velcro does not fray

Beret & Beret Pin

(Also known as 'cap badges')
Pronounciation: 'Berry' or 'Beré'
  • Must be worn when not under cover
  • Beret must be seasoned
  • Beret pin is aligned directly above left eye, use your fingures to gauge
  • Beret is to be folded to the right-side
    • There is a slight 'fold' above the beret pin when this happenes.
  • Fold beret into thirds and stow under right epaulette when told to

CPBS- and other badges

CPBS badges must be worn unless 'operational' variant is prescribed.

Same applies to badges worn above the left side, 1 cm above the breastpocket line.

Name tag is to be worn parallel to the breastpocket.

Boots

Boots must be shined (polished). This is especially for the toe-box of the shoe but if you somehow can polish your whole boot then do it.

You must use wax polish. Don't use instant polish – it will damage your boots.

Instant polish melts off much easier – so it's easy to spot. Anyone caught using instant polish deserves punishment.
